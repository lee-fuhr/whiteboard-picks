Whiteboard questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best whiteboard for you depends on how you plan to use it: melamine boards are budget-friendly for light use, porcelain surfaces last longer and resist stains, and glass boards offer a sleek look with no ghosting. Measure your wall space and consider whether you need magnetic functionality before you buy.

What are the main types of whiteboards and which is best?

The three most common types are melamine, porcelain (sometimes called enamel), and glass. Melamine is the most affordable option, but it tends to stain and ghost over time, great for temporary or low-traffic use like a child’s room or personal office. Porcelain boards are fired onto a steel surface, making them extremely durable, easy to erase, and often magnetic. They cost more upfront but can last for years without ghosting. Glass whiteboards are the premium choice: they never stain, wipe clean effortlessly, and look modern. They are also magnetic if backed with steel. However, they are heavy and significantly more expensive. For a busy conference room or classroom, porcelain is the practical sweet spot. For a home office where aesthetics matter, glass is worth the investment.

What size whiteboard should I buy?

Size is driven by your wall space and how far your audience will be. A common rule is that the board size should be about one-sixth to one-quarter of the wall it hangs on. For a small home office or cubicle, a board in the range of 2 to 3 feet wide works well. In a conference room, you will want something larger, often 4 feet wide or more, so that everyone seated can read the content. Don’t forget to account for height: mount the board so the center is at eye level for the primary user. If you are planning to write while standing, that is roughly 50 to 55 inches from the floor. Larger boards may require two people to install safely.

Do I need a magnetic whiteboard?

Magnetic functionality adds a lot of convenience if you plan to post notes, papers, or lightweight props. Most porcelain boards have built-in magnetism because they are bonded to a steel substrate. Many glass boards also offer magnetic backings, though not all. Melamine boards are not naturally magnetic; you can find melamine boards with a steel laminate, but they are less common. Think about how you work. If you frequently reference printed documents, use flashcards, or need to attach reminders, a magnetic board saves you from taping or pushing pins into the surface. For pure writing and erasing, non-magnetic works just fine. Just be aware: if you later decide you need magnetism, replacing the board is costly.

How do I keep my whiteboard clean and prevent ghosting?

Ghosting happens when ink residue embeds into the surface pores. The best prevention is using high-quality dry-erase markers and erasing daily. Ghosting appears most often on melamine boards because of their porous finish. Porcelain and glass surfaces resist ghosting naturally. If you see light stains, a dedicated whiteboard cleaner or a simple mixture of isopropyl alcohol and water works well. Avoid abrasive cleaners, acetone, or harsh chemicals, they can damage the board surface. Erase with a soft cloth or felt eraser. Some people also use a dry-erase marker to “re-wet” old marks and then erase them again, which can lift stubborn residue.

Can I paint a wall with whiteboard paint instead of buying a board?

Whiteboard paint is a legitimate alternative, especially if you need a custom shape or a very large surface. The paint creates a hard, glossy finish that is writable and erasable. However, the texture is rarely as smooth as a factory-made board, which can make marks harder to erase completely. Ghosting is more common with paint, and you’ll need to lightly sand the wall before applying it for good adhesion. Costwise, paint can be cheaper than a large porcelain board, but it does not come with a magnetic surface unless you separately add magnetic primer. Application takes careful preparation and several coats. If you are a renter, this might not be an option. For most buyers, a dedicated board is simpler and more reliable.

How should I mount my whiteboard?

Most framed whiteboards come with a mounting kit that includes horizontal wall brackets and screws. For plaster or drywall, you must anchor into studs or use heavy-duty toggle bolts, boards can be surprisingly heavy, especially large glass or porcelain models. Unframed glass boards often use adhesive pads or a keyhole bracket system; double-check the weight rating. Position the board so it is level and at a comfortable writing height. If the board is very large, have a helper hold it while you mark the screw holes. Some people install a marker tray below the board; these are usually sold separately. Always use a level and measure twice.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use dry-erase markers on a glass whiteboard?

Yes, glass whiteboards are designed for dry-erase markers. In fact, the non-porous surface makes them the easiest type to erase, most marks wipe off with a dry cloth. Just avoid permanent markers, as they may require solvent to remove from glass (though they do come off).

What markers are best for whiteboards?

Low-odor, fine- or chisel-tip dry-erase markers are the standard. Brands like Expo are widely available and reliable. Avoid bargain markers, which can leave more residue. For glass or porcelain boards, any quality dry-erase marker works; for melamine, choose markers labeled as “low odor” or “low ghosting” to extend the board’s life.

How do I remove permanent marker from a whiteboard?

Trace over the permanent marker with a dry-erase marker, let it sit for a few seconds, then wipe. The solvent in the dry-erase ink lifts the permanent ink. For stubborn marks, use a bit of isopropyl alcohol. Avoid acetone or nail polish remover, which can damage board surfaces.

Is a whiteboard better than a chalkboard?

Whiteboards produce no dust, are cleaner, and work with vibrant markers. Chalkboards require chalk dust that can aggravate allergies and need more maintenance. However, chalkboards are cheaper and work well in very bright rooms where whiteboards may glare. For most modern offices and homes, a whiteboard is the preferred choice.

Can I use a whiteboard in a home office?

Absolutely. A small to medium whiteboard mounted near your desk is great for brainstorming, task lists, and visual thinking. A glass board can double as a decorative element. Just be mindful of glare from overhead lights or windows, position it to avoid reflections.

How long does a whiteboard typically last?

A well-cared-for porcelain or glass board can last 10 to 20 years or more. Melamine boards may show ghosting within 1 to 3 years of heavy use and may need replacement sooner. The board’s life depends on the marker quality, cleaning habits, and how often it is used.

In shortChoosing the right whiteboard comes down to balancing budget, durability, and aesthetics. Porcelain boards are the workhorse of conference rooms, glass boards shine in design-conscious settings, and melamine offers an inexpensive entry point. Measure your space, decide on magnetic features, and commit to proper cleaning to get the most out of your purchase.
